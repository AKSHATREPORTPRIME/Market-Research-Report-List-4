<p><p>Snow sleds for children come in two main market types: steerable sleds and non-steerable sleds. Steerable sleds, as the name suggests, allow the child to control the direction by using a steering mechanism. These sleds offer more control and maneuverability on the snow. On the other hand, non-steerable sleds do not have a steering mechanism and rely on the child's body movement for direction. They are simpler in design and better suited for younger children who may struggle with steering.</p></p>
