<p><p>Extracorporeal Life Support System (ECLS) is a type of life-saving medical intervention that provides temporary support to the heart and lungs of patients with severe respiratory or cardiac failure. The two main types of ECLS systems are VA (Veno-Arterial) and VV (Veno-Venous). VA ECLS supports both the heart and lungs by removing blood from a vein, oxygenating it, and returning it to an artery. VV ECLS focuses on supporting only the lungs by removing and oxygenating blood from a vein before returning it to the same vein.</p></p>
