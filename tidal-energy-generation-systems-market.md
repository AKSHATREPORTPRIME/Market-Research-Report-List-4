<p><p>Tidal energy generation systems can be classified into three main types: coastal systems, river systems, and others. Coastal systems harness the power of ocean tides through structures like barrages and tidal turbines. River systems utilize the kinetic energy of tidal currents in rivers and estuaries. The "others" category includes innovative technologies such as tidal kites and vortex-induced vibrations. Each type of tidal energy generation system has its own advantages and challenges, offering diverse opportunities for sustainable energy production.</p></p>
