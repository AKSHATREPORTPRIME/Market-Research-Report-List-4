<p><p>Condensing units are key components in refrigeration and air conditioning systems responsible for removing heat from the system. There are three main types of condensing units based on the method of heat removal: air-cooled condensing units use air to dissipate heat, water-cooled condensing units use water for heat exchange, and evaporative condensing units use water and air to remove heat. Each type has its own advantages and applications, providing different solutions for various cooling needs.</p></p>
