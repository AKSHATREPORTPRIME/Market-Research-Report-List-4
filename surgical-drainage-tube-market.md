<p><p>Surgical drainage tubes are classified into two main types: passive drains and active drains. Passive drains rely on gravity to remove excess fluids from the body, while active drains use suction to facilitate drainage. The market for passive drains is driven by their simplicity and cost-effectiveness, while the market for active drains is growing due to their ability to remove fluids more efficiently and reduce the risk of infection. Both types of drainage tubes play a crucial role in post-operative care to promote healing and prevent complications.</p></p>
