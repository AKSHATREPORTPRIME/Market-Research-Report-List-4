<p><ul><li>Rotary Distributor Pump</li><li>Individual Control Pump</li><li>Unit Injection</li><li>Common Rail System</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Injection pumps are a vital component in diesel engines for delivering fuel to the combustion chamber. Different types of injection pumps include Rotary Distributor Pump, Individual Control Pump, Unit Injection, and Common Rail System. Rotary Distributor Pumps distribute fuel to each cylinder through a single rotating element. Individual Control Pumps provide individual control of fuel delivery to each cylinder. Unit Injection pumps have a separate pump for each cylinder. Common Rail Systems use a shared fuel rail to deliver fuel to all cylinders, offering precise control and improved efficiency.</p></p>
