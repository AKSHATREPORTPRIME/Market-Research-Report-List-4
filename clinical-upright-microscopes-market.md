<p><p>Clinical upright microscopes come in three main types: monocular, binocular, and trinocular. Monocular microscopes have one eyepiece, making them suitable for basic applications. Binocular microscopes have two eyepieces, providing a more comfortable viewing experience and allowing for better depth perception. Trinocular microscopes have three eyepieces, with the third one typically used for attaching a camera for image capture or sharing. Each type caters to different needs and preferences in the clinical setting.</p></p>
