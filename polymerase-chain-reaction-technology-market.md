<p><p>Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) technology is widely used for amplifying DNA samples. Two main types are Digital PCR and Real-Time PCR. Digital PCR allows for absolute quantification of DNA samples by partitioning them into thousands of individual reactions. Real-Time PCR, on the other hand, allows for monitoring amplification of DNA in real-time, enabling quantification and analysis as the reaction progresses. Both types have their own advantages and applications in research, diagnostics, and other fields.</p></p>
