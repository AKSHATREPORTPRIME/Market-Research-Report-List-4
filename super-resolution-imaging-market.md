<p><p>Super-Resolution Imaging refers to techniques that break the diffraction limit of light microscopy, allowing for higher resolution and greater detail in imaging. Types include STED (Stimulated Emission Depletion), PALM (Photoactivated Localization Microscopy), STORM (Stochastic Optical Reconstruction Microscopy), SIM (Structured Illumination Microscopy), and FPALM (Fluorescence Photoactivation Localization Microscopy). These techniques use various methods such as depleting fluorescence or activating specific molecules to achieve super-resolution imaging, enabling researchers to study biological phenomena at a more detailed level.</p></p>
