Stress Myocardial Perfusion Imaging involves the patient exercising or receiving a pharmacological stress agent to increase blood flow to the heart, while Resting Myocardial Perfusion Imaging is performed while the patient is at rest. Both types of MPI testing provide valuable information about the blood flow to the heart and can help doctors diagnose conditions such as coronary artery disease.</p></p>
