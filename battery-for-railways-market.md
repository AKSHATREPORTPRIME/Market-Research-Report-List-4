<p><p>Lead-acid batteries are the most commonly used in railways due to their low cost and reliability. They are suitable for backup power and emergency lighting systems. Li-Ion batteries are gaining popularity in the railway industry for their high energy density and longer lifespan. They are used for traction and auxiliary power in trains. Ni-Cd batteries are also used in railways for their high discharge rates and temperature resistance. They are commonly used in signaling systems and on-board equipment.</p></p>
