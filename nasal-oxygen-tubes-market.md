<p><p>Nasal Oxygen Tubes are categorized into two main types - Low-flow and High-flow Nasal Oxygen Tubes. Low-flow Nasal Oxygen Tubes deliver oxygen at a rate lower than the patient's peak inspiratory flow rate, typically ranging from 1-6 liters per minute. On the other hand, High-flow Nasal Oxygen Tubes provide oxygen at a higher flow rate, exceeding the patient's peak inspiratory flow rate, often ranging from 20-60 liters per minute. Both types of tubes are used for delivering oxygen therapy to patients with different respiratory needs.</p></p>
