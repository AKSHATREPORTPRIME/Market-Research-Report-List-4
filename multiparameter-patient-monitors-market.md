<p><p>Multiparameter patient monitors are categorized into high-acuity monitors, mid-acuity monitors, and low-acuity monitors based on the level of patient care they are designed for. High-acuity monitors are used in intensive care units and operating rooms to monitor critical patients requiring continuous vital signs monitoring. Mid-acuity monitors are used in step-down units and emergency rooms for patients with less critical conditions. Low-acuity monitors are typically utilized in general wards and outpatient settings for stable patients requiring minimal monitoring. Each type of monitor caters to specific patient care needs and acuity levels.</p></p>
